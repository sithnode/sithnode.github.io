About

I build business systems that make data trustworthy, useful, and operational.

My work sits at the intersection of data engineering, enterprise systems, financial technology, and execution leadership. I spend most of my time turning disconnected tools, reporting gaps, and process friction into systems leaders can actually rely on.

Background

I started in reporting and operational analysis at Investor's Business Daily, where I learned an early lesson that still shapes my work: data only matters if it reaches the right person in the right format at the right time.

That foundation led me into hedge fund operations and business systems at O'Neil Global Advisors, where I worked across GIPS compliance, portfolio platforms, reporting architecture, and fund data flows spanning SQL Server and PostgreSQL.

At Viking River Cruises, that same systems mindset expanded into payment integrations, Databricks pipelines, reporting modernization, and coordination across bank processors, third-party vendors, finance, and internal engineering teams.

Current Focus

My center of gravity is data engineering: pipelines, warehousing, reporting platforms, and the operating processes around them. I am interested in the full path from raw source data to decision-ready outputs.

Alongside that, I am building with AI agents, local LLM tooling, retrieval systems, and knowledge architecture. The goal is not novelty. The goal is to design systems that reduce coordination cost, improve clarity, and let people make better decisions faster.

How I Operate

Systems First

Tools matter less than the operating model they serve. I start with data flow, ownership, and decision points before choosing implementation details.

Trust Over Volume

A dashboard, data mart, or integration that leaders cannot trust creates more damage than silence. Reliability is part of the product.

Execution Across Functions

The hard part is usually not the technology. It is aligning engineering, finance, compliance, operations, and vendors around one clear implementation path.

Modernize With Purpose

I care about practical upgrades: replacing brittle legacy workflows, improving observability, and putting better information in front of decision-makers.
